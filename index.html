I have been working at Koa Health for over three years now, a company that spun out of Telefonica’s Innovation unit in November 2020. Currently I am overseeing the Digital Phenotyping research team and am focusing on capturing user behaviour from active/passive sensing data. I am interested in analysing data from our connected devices, as these data can unlock our ability to understand ourselves, which is the first step for improving who we are.

Previous to Koa, I was a principal investigator and team lead at IBM Ireland for over three years, mainly focusing on patents, publications and funding. To date, I have published over 25 publications in top venues, such as Ubicomp, PAKDD, ECML-PKDD, ICTAI, ACM TIST, and have filed over 20 patents. During this time, I was lecturing at NCI courses related to databases and big data part of the Higher Diploma programmes. I completed my PhD at University College Dublin, done in collaboration with IBM Ireland, where I focused on analyzing data from relational databases and scaling it up or down while maintaining representativeness or at fast speed. I completed my Bachelor in Computer Science and Engineering from "Politehnica" University of Timisoara, Romania .

I draw my experience from a mix of international programmes from Europe — Erasmus at UPM Spain, a summer research internship at DCU Ireland, a brief BEST (Board of European Students of Technology) summer school in Poland, PhD at UCD Ireland — to South America — a one year AIESEC student placement as a software developer in a start-up company in Brazil.
